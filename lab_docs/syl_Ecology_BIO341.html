Introduction

Learning Objectives

The learning objectives represent a set of skills that students are expected to develop through participation in this course.

Students will:

  1. Understand the major concepts of ecology including organismal, population, community, and ecosystem ecology.
  2. Understand how data from observations and experiments are used to develop and support ecological theory and concepts.
  3. Be able to effectively communicate scientific information in a written format.
  4. Be able to effectively use primary literature (i.e., scientific papers) as a resource to increase understanding.
  5. Understand how quantitive data and statistics are used to develop and support ecological theory.

Evaluation and Grading

The final grade in the course will be determined based on a combination of 2 exams (a midterm and a cumulative final exam), a research paper, the laboratory grade, and other class assignments (e.g., in–class writing). Additional information about each of these forms of evaluation will be provided as the class progresses and their relative contribution toward the final grade is shown below. No grades will be curved and there are no opportunities for extra credit.

Exams

The midterm and final exams will be take–home exams. Additional details will be provided at the time of the exams but you may use any published materials to assist you in completing the exam. You will not be allowed to receive help from any person during the exam, including internet–based question and answer sites.

Research Paper

You will be required to do a reseach paper on a topic relevant to the material covered in the course. Details about the format and grading of the paper will be provided during the course.

Reading and Other Class Assignments

Readings from Schlesinger 1997 are indicated in the lecture schedule above. You are expected to have read and understood the assigned section of the text before coming to class. We will be using the information covered in the readings during the lecture portion of the class but information in the readings may not be presented during the lecture. You are responsible for all of the material presented in the readings and during lecture. Most readings will be accompanied by a short writing assignment to be completed either during or before the class meeting. The details of these assignments will be provided as the course progresses.

Frequently, readings outside of the text will be assigned on the course Blackboard site. You are expected to have read and understood these additional readings before class. Just like the readings from the text, you are responsible for the material in the additional readings even if they are not covered in lecture.

Lab

Participation in the lab component of the course is mandatory. You will be given a lab syllabus during the first meeting of the lab.

Classroom Policies

Attendance

Your attendance and active participation in class activities is expected. Education does not result due to the passive transfer of information from professor to student but rather from the active acquisition of information and understanding in a supportive environment. I will work hard to provide you with the tools and environment you need to develop your understanding of environmental science and I will expect you to work hard to actively develop your understanding and facilitate the learning of your classmates. As such, I expect much more than your simple presence in the class. In–class assignments cannot be made–up.

Expectations

I will not tolerate any activities that show any level of disrespect toward other students, myself, or any other members of the Longwood or Farmville community.

I will expect professional conduct from all students at all times. This includes paying attention when I, or other members of the class are speaking. Cell phone use or computer use unrelated to classroom activities is not permitted.

It is extremely disruptive when students arrive late or leave early. Please make sure you are on time and ready to work at the beginning of each class period.

All email correspondence with myself or any other members of community should adhere to the standards of professional communication and include an appropriate greeting, properly formatted grammar and spelling, and a salutation.

I expect that all members of the class will adhere to the Longwood Honor Creed and Pledge at all times. If you are unclear on whether something is academically honest, be sure to ask. No one will hold it against you if you try to clarify your understanding of academic integrity.

Correspondence

All official correspondence for this course will occur through the Blackboard Site or through Longwood's email system. Please be sure to check both of these at least once a day for information regarding the class.

I am available without an appointment during my office hours (listed above) and I will respond to emails within a timely manner during normal school operation hours. If you cannot meet during any of the posted office hours, please email me to make an appointment.
